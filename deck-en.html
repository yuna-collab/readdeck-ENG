The legacy LIS, in 2026

Built before AI. Built before everything that matters now.

Today's leading lab information systems were architected in the 1990s. They store data — they don't reason on it. They were never designed for voice, for AI, or for the way a modern pathology practice actually works.

01

Built before AI

Legacy LIS can store data — they can't reason on it. AI is bolted on, not native.

02

1990s-style UX

Hostile forms, dozens of clicks per case. Pathologists fight the software instead of using it.

03

Disconnected from voice

Dictation lives in a separate tool. Transcription is a manual review-and-paste job.

04

No native synoptic / CAP

CAP-compliant synoptic reporting requires bolt-on tools or manual templates.

05

Closed integrations

LIS ↔ EHR ↔ scanners is a custom project every time. HL7/FHIR is half-implemented.

06

Reporting as afterthought

Statistics and operational insight are separate licences, separate dashboards, separate vendor.

07

Years to deploy

Multi-year rollouts. Six-figure change requests. Locked-in for a decade.
